Why Quantum-Resistant Keys Matter

Current Security: RSA Encryption

Modern digital security relies heavily on RSA encryption. It keeps information safe by using two very large prime numbers, making it extremely difficult for classical computers to break.

The Quantum Threat

Quantum computers use Shor's Algorithm to factor large numbers dramatically faster. A sufficiently powerful quantum computer could break RSA encryption in minutes, not millennia.

"A sufficiently powerful quantum computer could break RSA encryption in hours, putting much of today's secure communication at risk."
